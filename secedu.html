    Secondary Education

    The quality of secondary education is critical for a successful career.

    Secondary education expands and improves the students’ mental and physical faculties so that they may play their role as useful citizens of Pakistan.

    Grade 6-8

    Our Years 6–8 schools are safe, supportive, and engaging learning environments. We follows both UK and Pakistan curriculums to ensure academic excellence and holistic growth.

    Students may choose between the CAIE O-Level track (completed in Year 11) or the Matric programme (completed in Year 10). We offer personalised academic support where needed.

    Beyond academics, we emphasize values, emotional well-being, and strong parent-teacher partnerships. Sports and co-curricular activities are also encouraged to support well-rounded development.

    O Levels

    Overview

    We are confident that we can provide you with an environment that is conducive to learning, designed to stimulate your curiosity and challenge your understanding as you embark on your educational career.

    Our O Level programme offers a wide range of subjects through Cambridge Assessment International Education (CAIE), EdTech driven learning tools, and global learning resources.

    Simultaneously, students are engaged in extensive co-curricular activities, youth programmes and global interactions. Our dedicated team of highly qualified teachers and on-campus career counsellors work in close collaboration with parents to provide a holistic learning experience for students, enabling them to make better-informed career decisions that maximise individual capabilities and workplace needs.

    Counselling, Alumni & Placement Services (CAPS)

    • The CAPS team works for the holistic development of our students in Classes 8 to 11 by providing opportunities for self-development, for example, volunteer work, internships, participation in local and international competitions, and summer programmes.
    • The Guidance Counsellor at school evaluates students’ abilities and interests through aptitude assessments, counselling sessions and individual education plans.
    • The Guidance Counsellor works with the instructor, student and parents to choose the right subject combinations, thus ensuring the long-term career success of our TCS students.

    Matriculation

    Overview

    Little Folks School has a legacy of producing brilliant matriculation results throughout the country.

    Our dedicated faculty and holistic curriculum are the key to our success. We also provide students with opportunities to thrive in their passions for sports, music, and drama. Our Alumni include a long list of professional persons including doctors, engineers, lawyers, sportsmen, architects, businessmen, and entrepreneurs.

    The curriculum usually includes a combination of eight courses, including electives (such as Biology, Chemistry, Computing and Physics) as well as compulsory subjects (such as Mathematics, English, Urdu, Islamic studies and Pakistan Studies).

    Key features of our matriculation program.

    • Admissions to leading Engineering & Medical Universities
    • High Achievers in Board Examinations
    • Development of reading and writing skills
    • Competitive sports activities throughout the year
    • Role Play – Developing confidence and leadership qualities
    • English language as a preferred medium of exchange

    A Levels

    Overview

    Little Folks School is far from being a Shakespearean comedy though we believe that students should explore and reflect on their interests, find out who they are and who they can be.

    All will end well, due to the support given to students as they go through the highs and lows of their studies, extra-curricular activities and university placement with our dedicated college counsellors.

    We have currently the best A-Level results in the country, the year 2021 seeing a record breaking 39 World Distinctions and Awards.

    We offer a variety of scholarships for talented students.

    Where Champions Are Made

    Every student at Little Folks School is unique in their abilities and talents. Hence our school facilities are tailored to meet the diverse requirements of our student body in terms of their physical and emotional health.

    Science Laboratories

    Our Cambridge-standard labs are fully equipped and regularly updated to meet CAIE syllabus requirements.

    Computer Laboratories

    Our computer labs, equipped with modern technology and expert staff, meet Cambridge International standards with full networking and internet access.

    Libraries

    Students enjoy a rich collection of classic and modern stories, picture books, rhymes, story sacks, and puppets.

    Common Rooms

    Well-equipped common rooms offer A Level students a comfortable space with audio-visual, educational, and recreational facilities.

    Sports and Fitness

    Students develop their skills through indoor games and outdoor sports, guided by qualified national coaches and supported by well-equipped gyms and playgrounds.

    Cafeteria

    Our A Level campus features air-conditioned, hygienic cafeterias offering a variety of affordable meals.


    Counselling, Alumni & Placement Services

    Counselling, Alumni and Placement Services (CAPS) is a key part of our A Level programme. Experienced counsellors offer individual and group guidance on subject choices, college applications, and test preparation.

    Through the BridgeU platform, students access global career advice and expert insights. CAPS also supports skill-building through internships and volunteer work, helping students secure top local and international university placements.

    Fee Structure & Scholarships

    • The A Level fee structure varies from campus to campus and is based on the elective subjects opted by the students.
    • Little Folks School offers merit, sports and co-curricular scholarships. These range from a 25% to 100% waiver on the tuition fee.
    • Merit scholarships are based on a student’s O Level results or equivalent.
    • Little Folks School reserves the right to withdraw any schoae rules governing the scheme at any time at its sole discretion.

    Please contact your nearest Little Folks School – A Level Campus for more details.
